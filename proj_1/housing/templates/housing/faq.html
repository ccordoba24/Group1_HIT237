Frequently Asked Questions

Below are common questions and answers about the NT Housing Maintenance System. This system is designed to help tenants in Northern Territory remote communities report housing repair issues and allows staff or admin users to track, update, and manage those requests efficiently.

General Information

What is this system used for?

This system helps tenants report housing repair and maintenance issues and allows NT Housing staff and admin users to track, update, and manage those requests in one place. It provides transparency and efficiency in managing repairs across remote communities in the Northern Territory.

Who can submit a repair request?

Any logged-in tenant can submit repair requests for housing issues in their dwelling. Registered users must have a valid NT Housing tenant account. If you don't have an account, please contact your local community housing office or register through this system.

What types of repairs can I report?

You can report a wide range of maintenance and repair issues including:

  • Plumbing issues (leaks, blocked drains, no water)
  • Electrical problems (faulty outlets, flickering lights)
  • Air conditioning and heating system failures
  • Structural damage (broken walls, roof damage)
  • Door and window repairs (broken locks, damaged frames)
  • Flooring issues (broken tiles, damaged linoleum)
  • Appliance repairs (stove, refrigerator, washing machine)
  • Safety hazards and security concerns
  • General maintenance (painting, caulking, weatherproofing)

Making a Repair Request

How do I submit a repair request?

Log in to the system, click on "Repair Requests" in the dashboard, then select "New Request". Fill in the details about your repair issue, select the category that best describes your problem, and submit. You will receive a confirmation and can track the status of your request at any time.

What information do I need to provide when making a request?

Please provide the following information to help staff respond quickly:

  • Your dwelling unit number or address
  • Category of the repair needed
  • Detailed description of the problem
  • Severity level (if applicable)
  • When the problem started (if known)
  • Any urgent safety concerns

Can I submit an emergency repair request?

Yes. If you have an urgent safety issue (such as no water, no electricity, security breach, or safety hazard), submit your request immediately and mark it as urgent. For life-threatening emergencies, call emergency services first (000) before submitting a request. NT Housing staff will prioritize urgent repairs and respond as quickly as possible.

Tracking Your Request

Can tenants see the progress of their repair request?

Yes. You can view the current status of your repair requests at any time by logging into your account and going to the "Repair Requests" section. Status categories include: Pending, In Progress, Completed, and On Hold.

What do different repair statuses mean?

  • Pending: Your request has been received and is waiting to be assigned to a tradesperson.
  • In Progress: A tradesperson has been assigned and is working on your repair.
  • On Hold: The repair has been temporarily paused (e.g., waiting for parts or further information).
  • Completed: The repair has been finished and the issue should be resolved.

Can I receive updates about my repair?

Yes. You can check your repair status anytime through the system. Staff may also add maintenance updates with progress notes. You can view the full history of updates and maintenance work on your repair detail page.

How long will my repair take?

Repair timelines vary depending on several factors:

  • Emergency/urgent repairs: Priority response (same day or next day where possible)
  • High priority repairs: 3-7 days
  • Standard repairs: 1-3 weeks
  • Special order repairs (requires parts): 2-4 weeks
In remote NT communities, repair times may be longer due to travel distance and parts availability. Staff will update you if delays are expected.

Tenant Rights and Responsibilities

Can tenants edit their repair request?

Yes. You can edit basic details of your request (such as description and category) until a tradesperson has been assigned. Once work has started, you cannot change the request details through the system. Please contact NT Housing staff directly if you need to make changes to an in-progress repair.

Can tenants cancel a repair request?

You can withdraw a request that is still pending. However, once a tradesperson has been assigned or work has begun, you must contact NT Housing staff to discuss cancellation. Repeated cancellations or failure to allow access for repairs may affect your tenancy.

Can tenants update the repair status?

No. Only NT Housing staff and authorized tradespeople can update the official repair status. This ensures accurate tracking and maintains accountability for all maintenance work.

Can tenants add maintenance updates?

No. Maintenance updates are added only by NT Housing staff or authorized tradespeople. This ensures the repair history stays accurate and reliable. If you have concerns about the work, please contact your local housing office directly.

Do I need to provide access to my dwelling for repairs?

Yes. Tenants must allow reasonable access to their dwelling for repairs and maintenance. Please work with staff to schedule a convenient time. Failure to provide access may delay your repairs or result in the request being closed.

Maintenance History

What is the maintenance history?

The maintenance history shows all completed repairs and maintenance work on your dwelling. This includes previous repairs made by NT Housing staff and helps document the condition and maintenance of your home over time.

Can I view maintenance history for my dwelling?

Yes. Click on "Maintenance History" in your dashboard to see all completed repairs and updates for your unit. This helps you track what work has been done and when.

Account and Security

How do I register for an account?

Click "Register" on the login page and enter your details. You will need a valid NT Housing tenant ID to complete registration. If you don't have a tenant account, contact your local community housing office to get one set up.

How can I reset my password?

Password reset is not available in this version of the system. If you forget your password, please contact the system administrator or your local housing office for assistance with account recovery.

Is my information secure?

Yes. This system uses secure login credentials and protects your personal information. Only authorized NT Housing staff can view your repair requests and housing information. Never share your password with anyone.

Costs and Responsibilities

Do I have to pay for repairs?

No. Repairs covered by NT Housing tenancy agreements are provided at no cost to tenants. This system is designed for housing maintenance tracking. Payment and billing are not managed through this app. Repairs required due to normal wear and tear are the responsibility of NT Housing.

What if the damage is my fault?

Some damage may be the tenant's responsibility depending on your lease agreement and the cause of the damage. Intentional damage or damage from tenant negligence may result in repair costs being charged to the tenant. Staff will advise you if this applies to your situation.

What if damage is caused by weather or natural events?

Damage caused by storms, cyclones, flooding, or other natural events is generally the responsibility of NT Housing and should be reported immediately as an emergency repair. These repairs are typically handled on a priority basis.

Information for Remote Communities

How does this system work in remote NT communities?

This system is designed to work across all Northern Territory communities, including remote areas. You can submit repair requests online. NT Housing will coordinate with local tradespeople and visiting contractors to complete repairs. Internet connectivity may vary in remote areas, so submit requests when you have connectivity.

What if I don't have reliable internet access?

If you have difficulty accessing the online system, please contact your local NT Housing office to submit a repair request by phone or in person. Staff can help you enter your request into the system.

How are repairs scheduled in remote areas?

In remote communities, repairs may be scheduled based on visiting tradesperson schedules or availability of local contractors. Emergency repairs are given priority. Staff will work with you to schedule repairs at a time that works for your community.

Getting Help

What if I need help using the system?

If you need assistance, please contact your local NT Housing office. Staff can help you navigate the system, submit requests, and answer questions about your repairs.

Who do I contact if I have a problem or complaint?

If you have concerns about a repair, the system, or service from NT Housing, please contact your local housing office or the main NT Housing office. Complaints will be reviewed and addressed in accordance with NT Housing policies.

How often is the system maintained?

The system is regularly maintained and updated to ensure it works properly. If you experience technical problems, please notify your local housing office so they can assist you or report the issue to the system administrator.
