Section 1. Terms and conditions of group work

* Group allocation will follow a basic separation of tasks as needed for the assignment. For example, different students will complete different pages of the website project. Also, different students will be allocated different parts of the research needed for our theme, Remote Housing Crisis, following the guide provided in the Unit notes. Marks should be similar to all students unless a student does not complete their task or fails to contribute fairly. Lecturers will be informed of a student not contributing to the project. 
* Communication will primarily take place on Microsoft teams to ensure visibility to lecturers. All members must respond within 24 hours. WhatsApp will be used for urgent communication. The team will also hold video meetings before checkpoint dates to discuss progress and upcoming tasks, following the schedule on Microsoft Teams.
* Task ownership will be assigned during  MS Team  meetings, with a backup person assigned for each task. Failure to complete the assigned task by any of the team member without a valid reason will be reported to the lecturer. 
* Each student should allow sufficient time for others to answer texts and questions. Consideration for others' work, commitments and availability should always be respected. However, if no response is received within 72 hours, the issue will be noted and escalated. 
* Conflict resolution should be handled in stages. Stage one is to talk directly to the other student. In stage two we can discuss issues during team video meetings. Then stage three and final stage is to inform the lecturer supervising the group, Shantanu Barua.
* We all commit to doing our own work. Where AI is used, for example to generate code or check the code for correctness, we will make a note attributing that part of the work to AI and which AI is used.
* All tasks will be tracked using GitHub Issues with assigned responsibilities and deadlines to ensure accountability.
* All work must be reviewed by at least one other team member before submission to ensure quality and consistency.
* Contribution will be monitored through GitHub commits and task completion. Members who do not contribute fairly may receive adjusted marks.
* Group decisions will be made through discussion and majority agreement. In case of unresolved disagreement, the lecturer will be consulted.
